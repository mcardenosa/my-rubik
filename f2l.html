F2L (First Two Layers)

In this step you solve the upper and middle layer of the Rubik's cube.

This step is the most intuitive and can be solved without using algorithms and with a lot of practice. One way to do this is to first build the top layer cross as in the first resolution step for beginners.
It is a common practice to place the first layer in the bottom face of the cube because this way we will have a better perspective of where the rest of the pieces to place are. Most of these movements are simple and come out with practice.
Then there will be 4 columns to solve, which we can solve by placing the corresponding pieces 2 by 2.
We will not provide any algorithms for this step.
